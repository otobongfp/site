Otobong Peter

Hi, I’m Otobong Peter — but you can call me Otee. I started my tech journey in 2019, tinkering with microcontrollers as a hardware intern. That hands-on experience with IoT sparked my passion for building things that solve real-world problems. For my undergrad thesis, I built a facial recognition–powered door lock system — an early step toward systems that blend software and the physical world.

In 2020, I led the Google Developer Student Club at Rivers State University, discovering the power of mentorship and community. I explored EVM and Cardano ecosystems, eventually joining ABCD Technologies as a co-founder, where I trained devs and scaled communities. I also led Nigeria’s Blacks in Technology chapter, which won Growth Chapter of the Year.

At LTO Network, I helped architect backend systems, client libraries to power the RWA ecosystem and decentralized ID solutions, like the EQTY Passport. I'm focused on distributed systems, resilient backends, and AI-powered tooling—still building for a post-AI world.

I hold a Bachelor’s in Electronics and I’m pursuing an M.Sc. in Software Engineering. My research spans agentic AI, adaptive UIs, dev tooling, and distributed systems. Outside code, I explore leadership, write, paint, dive into sci-fi, and build communities. Also, I'm a Christian.
