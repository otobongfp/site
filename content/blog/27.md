# 27

As I inch closer to another year, I have had to intentionally make time to think about where I am and where I am going. Not because I lack clarity, but because life moves so fast that it is easy to become a spectator in your own story.

There are always so many moving parts. So many projects. So many plans. So many relationships to manage. And somehow, you are expected to be great, or at least good enough, in every area while still carrying the responsibility of leading yourself.

I often think everybody experiences moments like this. But for a man especially, if you miss yourself at this stage of life, you can easily become everybody else's art project. You spend your years carrying burdens that were never yours and trying to live up to the expectations of people who genuinely mean well. Yet even good intentions can slowly shape you into a mosaic of other people's desires.

And before you realize it, your own dreams begin to die quietly.

You start performing a version of yourself that others are more comfortable with seeing. I think before physical death, this is one of the worst forms of death. Sadly, our culture rewards it.

In two days, I will turn 27. In three years, I will be 30. That realization came faster than I expected.

Lately, I have noticed something interesting. While destiny, time, and chance seem to quietly push me toward purpose, I also feel the weight of the expectations around me and the subtle need to please everyone. So I decided to pause for a while and observe myself honestly. I wanted to identify the parts of me that had been shaped by external pressure rather than conviction, and over the last few weeks I have been intentionally chiseling those parts away.

It can be argued that we are all shaped by many philosophies, and I agree. But I also believe you should never adopt a philosophy you have not deeply interrogated for yourself.

One thing I have come to accept is that my path in life is unusual. I have always been the idea person. The thinker. The strategist. The philosopher. I naturally live in ideas, systems, and possibilities. In many ways, that mindset became a form of self preservation for me.

But to truly live, a person must eventually move beyond idealizing and begin mastering execution.

For the last two years especially, that has been the skill I have been sharpening. The ability to move ideas from imagination to reality. From sketch to product. From possibility to structure.

I have accepted that my mission can be summarized in a few words: *quod tango auto*, "that which I touch, I change."

And I embrace that more deeply now than ever before.

My assignment is to create worlds that other people can live in. Whether that means building companies that create jobs and keep lights on in different homes, or creating software that improves people's lives, or building systems that outlive me, I know that transformation is central to who I am.

Everywhere I go, I find it difficult to sit still because my mind constantly notices what can be improved. I am no Midas, but I have always desired to see stone become gold.

So as I turn 27, my goals are simple:

To sharpen my execution.  
To lead myself better.  
To interrogate ideas before absorbing them.  
To master myself.  
To stay on my path without bending to every perception around me.  
To become more audacious.

The beautiful part of all this is that my 27th birthday will coincide with my time in Togo. It will be my first time crossing Nigeria's borders after spending the last 26 years of my life within them.

On many levels, that feels prophetic.

A reminder that I am stepping into a more audacious era of my life. A reminder that there is still so much more ahead of me than behind me.

Anything I can imagine, I can build.  
Anyone I can imagine, I can become.
