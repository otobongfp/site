# 27

Life moves fast, and it's easy to lose yourself in it. Between projects, plans, and people, you can end up living for everyone else instead of yourself. For men especially, this is a real danger. You start carrying burdens that aren't yours and chasing other people's expectations until your own dreams quietly fade away.

I'm turning 27 in two days. In three years, I'll be 30. That hit me faster than I expected.

Lately, I've been honest with myself. I noticed how much of who I am had been shaped by outside pressure rather than my own convictions. So I've been stripping those parts away.

I've always been a thinker, someone who lives in ideas and possibilities. But I've learned that thinking isn't enough. You have to execute. For the past two years, I've been focused on turning ideas into real things.

My mission, simply put: what I touch, I change. I want to build companies, software, and systems that improve people's lives and outlast me.

So as I turn 27, my goals are simple: execute better, lead myself well, question ideas before accepting them, and stay on my own path without bending to every opinion around me.

And there's something special about this birthday. I'll be spending it in Togo, maybe it's coincidence or just a divine ochestration. Regardless, cheers to a new chapter.

Wisdom to build, Humility to keep learning, and Patience to become.
